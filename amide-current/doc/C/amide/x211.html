Gotcha's to ROI calculations

Variance/Std. Dev./Std. Err. Fallacies

A common fallacy is to use the calculated variance (or standard deviation, or standard error) of an ROI as an experiment's variance. The variance of an experiment can only truly be measured by taking multiple samples (i.e. performing multiple scans) and calculating the variance between these different samples. The variance that AMIDE calculates is only a measure of the noise within an ROI in the given data set.
